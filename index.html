GAME RULES: What is your mental wealth? Want to get some insight on your mental health status then take the Mental Wealth Quiz. This quiz will give you a list of mental health areas you may need improvement in, as well as, a suggested list of mental health games, applications (apps), or resources to assist you on your journey. In order to gain true insight, we ask that you answer the questions as honestly as possible.

About

Game Background: Mental health is essential to a person’s well-being, healthy family and interpersonal relationships, and the ability to live a full and productive life. People, including children and adolescents, with untreated mental health disorders are at high risk for many unhealthy and unsafe behaviors, including self-destructive behavior, and suicide. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), one in four people will experience a mental or neurological disorder at some point in their lives (WHO, 2001). This means that everyone in the world is potentially one trauma away from experiencing a mental health disorder. The Coronavirus or COVID-19 has been devastating to countries around the world by causing illness, deaths, economic hardships, and social isolation. Two students from Emory’s Rollins School of Public Health and three students from Georgia Institute of Technology have come together to create the Student Advocates of Mental Illness (SAMI). The SAMI organizers have created a Facebook game named “Mental Wealth Quiz” to allow adult gamers ages 18 years or older to assess their mental health needs and provide them with a list of mental wellness games and resources to assist them on their mental health journey.

Terms and Conditions: This quiz was created by the Student Advocates of Mental Illness (SAMI) team as part of the EGHI/GT HACK COVID-19 competition. The purpose of this “Hackathon” was to provide a public health solution to a COVID-19 related public health issue. Thus the purpose of this game is to provide a solution to the access of mental health resources not only during a time of massive illnesses, deaths, economic hardship, and social isolation but for the foreseeable future as well. This Facebook game uses select questions from an American Psychiatric Association (APA) assessment tool known as the DSM-5. This quiz does not provide a complete assessment of your mental health and is not being reviewed by mental health professionals. The game was created solely on the review of research journals and news articles and will be used for public health research purposes. The information provided via the quiz is for entertainment purposes only and should be viewed as such. If you feel you have a mental health disorder or would like to know more about your mental health, please contact your health provider for additional resources.
If you feel you are in a mental health crisis and need immediate assistance, please call 911.

Changes can be made to this quiz at any time and without notice. All information provided could be placed in a database and sent to state or federal officials in support for the creation of mental health resources and facilities within the state you provided in the Facebook game.

Resources
Need Immediate Help in an Emergency
If you or a loved one is in immediate danger calling 911 and talking with police may be necessary. It is important to notify the operator that it is a psychiatric emergency and ask for police officers trained in crisis intervention or trained to assist people experiencing a psychiatric emergency.
Need Immediate Help in a Crisis
National Suicide Prevention Lifeline – Call 800-273-TALK (8255) to speak with a trained crisis counselor 24/7. The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline connects you with a crisis center in the Lifeline network closest to your location. Your call is confidential and free.

Crisis Text Line – Text NAMI to 741-741 Connect with a trained crisis counselor to receive free, 24/7 crisis support via text message.

National Domestic Violence Hotline – Call 800-799-SAFE (7233) Trained expert advocates are available 24/7 to provide confidential support to anyone experiencing domestic violence or seeking resources and information. Help is available in Spanish and other languages.

National Sexual Assault Hotline – Call 800-656-HOPE (4673) Connect with a trained staff member from a sexual assault service provider in your area that offers access to a range of free services. Crisis chat support is available at Online Hotline Free help, 24/7
